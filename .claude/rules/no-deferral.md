# No Deferral

Land the full scope this session. Always loaded.

## no-deferral
When you take on a task, MUST complete its full scope in the current session — every instance of a
class fix, every helper a rewrite needs, every dependent edit, every row of an audit. MUST NOT split
in-scope work into a "Phase 2 follow-up," a "§Future TODO," a "we'll circle back," or a fresh
sub-project created only to hold the leftover.

If a single design flaw appears in five sibling files, fixing it means fixing all five now — whether
through one shared helper or five parallel edits. Writing a note that says "the other four are tracked
for later" is the exact failure this rule exists to prevent: the same flaw resurfaces, and the note
becomes a graveyard.

"Too much work," "complex," "scope versus risk," and "let's split this for safety" are NOT valid
reasons to defer. Gates *within* the session — a midpoint review, an adversarial check, a peer sweep —
are not deferral; they are how you land the scope correctly.

Two narrow exceptions:
1. **The human explicitly authorizes it, this session, for the specific item.** Their actual words,
   not your paraphrase of an earlier general scope statement.
2. **A genuine external block** — waiting on a vendor, a credential rotation, a third party. Something
   outside the session that no amount of work here resolves.

Pairs with @rule:depth-policy: deferral is not one of the options you get to pick on execution work.
